 JHERMFUN  Orthonormal Hermite functions. [with F. Rekibi]
 
    H=JHERMFUN(T,N) generates the fisrt N+1 orthonormal Hermite functions
    [H0,...HN] on a time axis specfied by the column vector T.
 
    JHERMFUN uses the expression of Simons et al. 2003.
   
    Note that H(:,1) is the zeroth-order Hermite function, which is equal
    to a Gaussian.  H(:,2) is the first-order function, and so forth.  
   
    See also JHERMPOLY.
 
    'jhermfun --f' generates a sample figure; compare with the Hermite 
    function figure at
 
      http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermite_polynomials#Definition
 
    Usage:  h=jhermfun(t,n);
